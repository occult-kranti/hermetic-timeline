A Chronology of Hermetic Philosophy, Magic & the Occult

As above,so below.

From Babylon and Egypt to the academy · transmission, translation & the line between record and rumour

The maxim above, paraphrased from the Emerald Tablet, became the signature of a tradition that crossed languages and empires — from the star-priests of Babylon and the scribes of Egypt, through Roman Alexandria and the Arabic translators of Harran, to Ficino's Florence, John Dee's angelic tables, and the magical orders of the modern age. This timeline follows that documented thread, and marks where popular history shades into conspiracy.

A note on method. This is a curated overview, not an exhaustive register, and dates for ancient and medieval texts are scholarly estimates rather than fixed points. The labels exist to keep a real distinction visible: the Hermetic tradition is a genuine, well-studied strand of intellectual history, while the modern “Illuminati bloodline” and “New World Order” narratives that borrow its imagery have no evidentiary basis and descend from a documented chain of fabrication — not from the sources catalogued here. Where history is uncertain, it is marked disputed; where a specific claim has been disproven, debunked.
